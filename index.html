About

Researching robots for complex real-world environments

I am a Ph.D. student at Beihang University. My research explores learning-based methods for autonomous robots, particularly systems that combine perception, decision-making, locomotion, and manipulation.

My current work spans quadruped loco-manipulation, reinforcement learning, embodied intelligence, autonomous unmanned aerial vehicles, multi-robot cooperation, and reliable deployment from simulation to real robotic platforms.

Research Interests

Core areas

My research connects robot learning with deployable autonomous systems across ground and aerial platforms.

01

Embodied Intelligence

Integrated perception, reasoning, decision-making, and control for robots interacting with dynamic physical environments.

02

Reinforcement Learning for Robotics

Policy learning, robust control, and adaptation for locomotion, manipulation, and whole-body robotic behaviors.

03

Legged Loco-Manipulation

Whole-body coordination of quadruped robots and manipulators for stable, accurate, and task-oriented physical interaction.

04

Autonomous Aerial Systems

UAV perception, navigation, trajectory planning, cooperative networking, and reliable operation in real environments.

05

Robot Perception and Navigation

LiDAR, vision, state estimation, mapping, and planning for autonomous operation in complex and unstructured spaces.

06

Sim-to-Real Transfer

Bridging simulation and hardware through robust learning, system identification, adaptation, and deployment-aware control.
